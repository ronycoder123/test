Baker From Goa

Tucked away in the peaceful villages of Goa is a charming figure who has remained a part of the region’s identity for generations — the traditional Goan baker, or poder. Long before sunrise, he begins his day by preparing batches of pão, poee, unddo, and kankonn in rustic, wood-fired ovens, following recipes passed down through his family. Riding through narrow, tree-lined lanes on a simple bicycle with a large bread basket and a horn that announces his arrival, the poder is greeted by locals eagerly waiting for their daily bread. His presence is more than just a convenience; it's a tradition that connects people to the past and to each other. Influenced by Portuguese culture and blended with Goan warmth, this bread-making art is deeply woven into the region’s daily rhythm, community spirit, and culinary heritage. From the crispy crusts to the soft interiors, every loaf tells a story of care, culture, and connection.
